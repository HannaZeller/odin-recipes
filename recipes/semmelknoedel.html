Semmelknödel

Description

Semmelknödel are amazing and one of my favourite foods (I have great taste).
They use old Semmeln (bread rolls) that are too old and dry to be enjoyed anymore and make them into a fabulous meal.
They can be a side dish to meat and a nice sauce, but they can also be the star of the whole meal in a nice creamy mushroom sauce for example.
Even on their own, cut into slices and pan fried in butter, they are absolutely delicious!

Ingredients

  • 6 old and dry Semmeln (bread rolls)
  • 3 tbsp. parsley (put lots)
  • 10g butter
  • 1 onion
  • 250ml milk
  • 3 eggs
  • salt and pepper
  • bread crumbs depending on consistency
  1. Cut the Semmeln into small cubes or thin slices. Put chopped parsley and onion into a pot with the butter and heat it up (but don't let it get brown), then mix with the bread cubes.
    Bring the milk almost to a boil and pour over the bread. Leave the mixture for 10 minutes.
  2. Mix the eggs with the salt and pepper and add them to the bread mixutre. Knead until it becomes a firm dough. If it is too wet, add bread crumbs.
  3. With wet hands form tennis ball sized Knödel and put them into hot (but not boiling) salt water. Wait for 20 minutes until the Knödel float to the surfice.
